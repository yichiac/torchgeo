PASTIS → Germany cross-domain inference

Notes on how pastis_to_germany_inference.py diverges from or interprets the informal spec. Updated as I work.

There are now two scripts that do the same job differently:

  • pastis_to_germany_inference.py — from-scratch version. STAC queries, band reading/warping, EuroCrops download and rasterization all hand-rolled. Easier to read top-to-bottom; nothing depends on torchgeo’s GeoDataset machinery.
  • pastis_to_germany_inference_torchgeo.py — simplified version that leans on torchgeo: Sentinel2(time_series=True), EuroCrops subclass with an overridden get_label, composed with & and sampled by GridGeoSampler. Less code in the main path, but adds a download-to-cache step.

What the script does

  1. Auto-discovers the most recent best checkpoint under outputs_full/lightning_logs/version_*/checkpoints/ and loads SpatioTemporalSegmentationTask from it.
  2. Queries Microsoft Planetary Computer (STAC) for Sentinel-2 L2A scenes covering a small German bounding box for the 2021 growing season, filters by cloud cover, and assembles a (T, C, H, W) time-series matching PASTIS's 10-band layout (B02, B03, B04, B05, B06, B07, B08, B8A, B11, B12).
  3. Tiles the AOI into non-overlapping 128×128 patches at 10 m and runs the ConvLSTM forward.
  4. Rasterizes EuroCrops DE_NRW_2021 polygons over the same grid and computes confusion matrix + per-class IoU/precision/recall, after mapping both label spaces to a small common crop set (see below).
  5. Saves a stitched RGB / prediction / label visualization to outputs_germany/.

Design decisions

Decision AOI: southwestern NRW (near Düren), not Karlsruhe

You picked “small fixed bbox near the Strasbourg/Karlsruhe border.” However, torchgeo.datasets.EuroCrops only ships two German subsets: DE_LS_2021 (Lower Saxony) and DE_NRW_2021 (North Rhine-Westphalia). Neither covers Baden-Württemberg, Rheinland-Pfalz, or Saarland. To keep quantitative evaluation against EuroCrops, I moved the bbox to southwestern NRW (around 50.80°N, 6.45°E, near Düren) — the closest available labelled region to France (∼150 km from the border, via Belgium/Luxembourg). Climatically and agriculturally it remains closer to PASTIS-R’s southern France distribution than, say, Lower Saxony.

Decision Year mismatch: use 2021 imagery to match 2021 labels

PASTIS-R covers 2018–2019 in France; EuroCrops DE_NRW is 2021. Crop rotations are year-specific, so labels and imagery must come from the same year. I use 2021 S2 imagery and accept the additional ∼3-year domain shift on top of the France→Germany shift. This is documented as a known limitation in the script’s header.

Decision 8-class common crop mapping

Decision ~38 Sentinel-2 timesteps, Sept 2020–Nov 2021

Training used padding_length=38, so I sample up to 38 lowest-cloud scenes from a 15-month window: 2020-09-01 to 2021-11-30. This matches PASTIS-R’s typical agricultural-year span (~Sept of year N−1 through Nov of year N) and lets the time series cover winter-crop emergence, overwintering, spring green-up, summer growth, and harvest — the full phenological cycle the ConvLSTM saw during training. If fewer than 38 cloud-free scenes survive, I pass the actual count via the lengths argument to ConvLSTM (variable-T) rather than zero-padding.

Decision 128×128 tiling, no overlap

Model was trained at 128×128. I tile the AOI into non-overlapping 128×128 chips and run them independently, then stitch predictions for visualization. No test-time augmentation, no overlap blending — keeps the script simple.

Decision AOI scaled up to 10.24 km (64 patches), then replicated

Initial AOI was 2.56 km (4 patches) near Düren — useful for the first smoke test, too small for meaningful per-class numbers. After the smoke test results looked reasonable, I moved the center to 51.90°N, 7.60°E (Münsterland, NRW) and increased size_m to 10240 (8×8 grid of 128×128 chips). Münsterland was chosen for its broader continuous farmland and richer crop mix than the rolling terrain around Düren.

Once that AOI confirmed the model generalizes, I added two more AOIs at the same 10.24 km size in adjacent parts of Münsterland (52.00°N, 7.85°E “east” near Warendorf; 51.80°N, 7.30°E “west” near Coesfeld). All three live in UTM 32N and inside DE_NRW_2021, so the script shares one model load and one EuroCropsCommon instance across them, then emits per-AOI figures named pastis_to_germany_{central,east,west}.png and a single combined metrics.txt.

Deviation Reverted EuroCrops to direct geopandas (likely the silent killer)

The torchgeo EuroCrops wrapper’s _check_integrity walks the full zenodo_files tuple (16 country zips) and falls back to _download() if any are missing — that’s a multi-GB download triggered just to access DE_NRW_2021. We strongly suspect this is what was killing the job silently (quota or wallclock cutoff, no Python traceback). Restored the targeted ensure_eurocrops helper that downloads only DE_NRW_2021.zip via torchgeo.datasets.utils.download_url, plus rasterize_eurocrops which reads the single shapefile through geopandas, reprojects, clips, and rasterizes with our 8-class hcat_to_common LUT. ~50 extra lines compared to the subclass approach, but reliable and fast for a single country.

Decision Six AOIs across NRW agricultural belts

Expanded from three Münsterland AOIs to six spanning the main agricultural regions of NRW: three in Münsterland (central, east, west), one on the Lower Rhine (niederrhein, vegetables/cereals), one in the Soester Börde (soest, wheat/sugar-beet loess belt), and one in the Rhineland near Düren (rhineland, the original smoke-test location). All six remain in UTM 32N and inside DE_NRW_2021. The model, the EuroCrops zip, and the on-disk shapefile are loaded once and shared across all AOIs.

Also kept: the log_mem(tag) diagnostic at four checkpoints per AOI (before S2 fetch, after S2 fetch, after inference, after EuroCrops rasterization), and the post-inference drop of the full time-series tensor (~1.5 GB float32), keeping only the first two frames for the RGB viz.

Decision Threaded band reads (cache removed)

The 10 PASTIS bands per scene are fetched concurrently via a ThreadPoolExecutor (10 workers). GDAL releases the GIL during I/O so this scales cleanly — expected speed-up ~5–10× on the network-bound part.

A per-AOI disk cache was added briefly to make reruns instant, but removed at user request (~750 MB per AOI is too much for the working storage). Every run does a fresh fetch.

Decision Visualization shows first two timesteps instead of median

Switched the leftmost panel from a season-median RGB composite to two RGB panels showing the first two timesteps in the input series (with acquisition dates in the titles). Reason: a median composite smooths over the very phenology the model keys on; seeing two real frames makes it obvious whether the input series starts at the right point in the season and isn’t cloud-contaminated.

Decision S2 reflectance normalization: divide by 10000

The PASTIS datamodule normalizes with mean=0, std=10000. Planetary Computer harmonizes S2 L2A to the pre-baseline-04 reflectance scale (0–10000), so the same divide-by-10000 normalization carries over without offset adjustments.

How the torchgeo version differs

Decision S2 cached locally with Sentinel-2 naming

torchgeo’s Sentinel2 reads files from disk via a filename regex (T{tile}_{date}_{band}.tif), so the script first downloads PASTIS bands from Planetary Computer, reprojects + clips them to the AOI at 10 m, and writes uint16 COGs into outputs_germany_tg/s2_cache/ using that naming. Subsequent runs skip already-cached files (this is the cheap caching the original script lacked).

Decision Common-class mapping via EuroCrops.get_label override

EuroCropsCommon overrides two methods: _load_class_map (to declare 8 classes so EuroCrops.plot’s color normalization works) and get_label (to return the common class index per polygon by string-matching EC_hcat_n). This is the cleanest extension point in VectorDataset’s rasterize loop. The hierarchical HCAT parent-code lookup used by stock EuroCrops is bypassed — we don’t need it because we match on the human-readable name.

Decision Composition via Sentinel2 & EuroCropsCommon + GridGeoSampler

The intersection dataset gives aligned image / mask pairs in a shared CRS. GridGeoSampler(size=128, stride=128, roi=…) emits the four non-overlapping chips that cover the 256×256 AOI; stack_samples collates. The main inference loop is just five lines.

Tradeoff Local disk cache vs streaming

The torchgeo path requires the imagery on disk, so the first run downloads ∼38 scenes × 10 bands ≈ 380 small COGs into the cache (each clipped to the AOI, so a few MB total — bandwidth and disk are not a concern at this AOI size). The tradeoff is one extra step (the download helper) for a much shorter main loop and automatic re-runs.

Open Should EuroCrops zip be downloaded by torchgeo or staged manually?

The script passes download=not exists(); on first run torchgeo will fetch the full Zenodo bundle (16 zips, ~GB). If you already have DE_NRW_2021.zip in $EUROCROPS_ROOT, set the env var and it will be picked up without a re-download. Confirm where you want the cache to live.

Deviations from the spec

Deviation AOI is in NRW, not at the French border

See AOI decision above — driven entirely by where torchgeo’s EuroCrops has German labels. If you want the Karlsruhe-area bbox, we can keep it but drop quantitative evaluation (would need to source labels separately, e.g. InVeKoS / LPIS for Baden-Württemberg).

Decision EuroCrops via a small EuroCrops subclass

The from-scratch script used to do the EuroCrops download, zip extraction, geopandas read, CRS reproject, polygon clip, and rasterio.features.rasterize by hand — about 50 lines. Replaced with a 10-line EuroCropsCommon subclass that overrides _load_class_map (so len(class_map) == 8) and get_label (which calls hcat_to_common on EC_hcat_n). A single bbox query — ec[xmin:xmax, ymin:ymax]['mask'] — returns the rasterized 8-class mask. Same subclass pattern as the torchgeo version; just keeps the S2 path PC-streaming instead of file-caching.

Tradeoffs considered

Tradeoff Planetary Computer vs CDSE

You chose PC. PC is auth-free and works headless on this cluster, but its S2 archive is harmonized to the old reflectance baseline and does not include all CDSE QA layers. We’re using eo:cloud_cover scene-level filtering only, not pixel-level SCL masking. For a small-N demo this is fine; for production you’d want SCL.

Tradeoff Median composite vs full T-series

Using a multi-date composite would let us reuse a non-temporal segmenter, but it throws away phenology — which is the whole reason PASTIS uses ConvLSTM. Stuck with the full time-series.

Tradeoff Cloudy-pixel handling

Cheapest: drop the whole scene if eo:cloud_cover > 20%. Done. Better: pixel-level SCL mask + temporal interpolation. Skipped to keep the script under one file — can revisit if results look noisy.

Open questions

Open Is southwestern NRW acceptable?

If you specifically need the bbox at the French border (e.g. for a slide), say so and I’ll add a second path that runs inference there without EuroCrops eval (qualitative only) in addition to the NRW eval.

Open 2021 imagery vs 2018 imagery

I chose 2021 to match EuroCrops labels. If you’d rather hold the imagery year constant to PASTIS’s training distribution (2018), we’d need 2018 labels for Germany — which torchgeo doesn’t have. Confirm 2021 is fine.

Open Sample size for the demo

Currently 4 tiles (256×256 area ≈ 6.5 km²). Good enough for a sanity check; too small to claim anything about average accuracy. Tell me a target area if you want a more statistically meaningful run.
